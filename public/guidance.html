Placement that feels natural

Layer your light

Combine ambient overhead lighting with task lights at work surfaces and accent LEDs to highlight textures or artwork. This layered approach creates depth and lets you adjust the mood throughout the day.

Aim away from eyes

Position strips and fixtures so the beam points toward surfaces, not directly into sight lines. Under-cabinet lights should angle down at counters; shelf strips work best tucked behind edges to graze walls rather than shine forward.

Let materials glow

Grazing light across textured walls, wood grain, or stone brings out natural beauty. Place LED strips close to surfaces—within an inch or two—to emphasize dimension and create soft shadows that add warmth and character.

Common questions, answered simply

We know lighting can feel technical. Here's plain-language help for the questions we hear most—no jargon, just clarity.

Is brighter always better?

Not necessarily. Too much brightness can feel harsh and tire your eyes. The right amount depends on the room's purpose—work spaces need more light, bedrooms less. We help you find the balance that feels just right.

What makes light feel warm?

Color temperature, measured in Kelvin. Lower numbers (2700K–3000K) give that cozy, golden glow like candlelight. Higher numbers (4000K+) feel crisp and energizing. Most homes blend both depending on the room.

How do I avoid glare?

Choose fixtures with diffusers or shades that soften the light. Position lights so they don't shine directly into your eyes. Indirect lighting—bouncing off walls or ceilings—creates a gentler, more comfortable atmosphere.

Do I need the same Kelvin everywhere?

No. Kitchens often benefit from cooler, focused light for tasks, while living rooms feel better with warmer tones. Mixing temperatures by room creates natural transitions and suits each space's mood.

What beam angle should I pick?

Narrow beams (15°–30°) spotlight specific areas like artwork. Wide beams (60°+) wash entire rooms with even light. Think about whether you want to highlight something or illuminate everything equally.

How do I balance cozy and functional?

Layer your lighting. Use ambient light for overall brightness, task lights where you work or read, and accent lights for warmth. Dimmers let you adjust the mood throughout the day without changing fixtures.
